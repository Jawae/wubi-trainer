What is Wubi?

  • 五笔字型输入法 (Wǔbǐ zì xíng shūrù fǎ) is a method to type chinese characters on a standard computer keyboard, invented by Wang Yongmin.
  • Wubi is a shape-based input method: Chinese Characters are identified by a sequence of character components.
    In general each character is identified by four keystrokes, but very common characters require less than four keystrokes or even have a one letter shortcut.

The advantages of the Wubi method (personal opinion)

  • You can write characters that you don't know the pronunciation and meaning of. (This is something that might be quite useful for language learners)
  • Paying more attention to how characters are composed might make it easier to remember and distinguish between them.
  • Typing becomes very automatic and fast: You don't have to select the right character out of a list of homophonic characters. The chinese characters are uniquely identified by their components and stroke order.

Requirements

  • It takes a while to memorize and practice the location of all the character components. (you need to build muscle memory so that the typing becomes automatic)
  • You should already be familiar with how chinese characters are composed in general
  • You need a basic knowledge how chinese stroke order works: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stroke_order#General_guidelines

How to use this app?

  1. Under "Lessons" select a set of character components to practice
  2. Then switch to the "Practice" page, click "start typing" and press the key for the prompted character
  3. When learning new character components use ENTER to show the wubi keyboard layout.
    You can also press SPACE to take a quick peek where this character component is located on your keyboard and to which group of components it belongs.

More tips...

  • Practice new character components slowly until you make few errors and know their position on the keyboard by heart. Then try to speed your typing up.
  • Start with individual characters sets, then combine several lists and practice them together
  • Compare the character components to the other ones on the same key, similar components are grouped together
  • Components with less strokes are located along the keyboard center
  • Your progress on each character is stored locally in your browser
  • More details about Wubi (including different versions) https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wubi_method
